Arks Of Octava

Jeramiah Poe isn’t just any character in the Realm of Fiction; he is Muse Master—Destiny Diviner—Mysterious Miskriat. Being of neither the Traditional Genre Provinces nor Independent, Poe enjoys an eternal lease on life, so long as his Scribbler keeps him out of publication.

Poe meets Kane, a seven-year-old boy from the Independent Horror Province, where he learns ancient codes are being broken and the horror that should be an act, is real.

But the evil clutching Octava is not new and Seven Arks have been sent to Earth to stop it. Only something has gone wrong and Poe is commissioned as the 8th Ark of Octava to discover what has become of the Seven.

But his passage to Earth comes with revelations he's not prepared for. Not only does his Scribbler not know of his existence, he's a she that his human form seems allergic to. Poe soon realizes that with each Ark he locates, his powers grow along with his feelings for the Scribbler. And the enemy will try and use both to gain control of the two realms.

I just finished reading this for the second time, why you ask? Because I was trying to figure out how to put into words how I felt about this book. Expletives...lots of expletives would be needed. Did you love Chronicles of Narnia? The NeverEnding Story? Alice in Wonderland? If you love those fantasy worlds with vibrant characters, Heroes and villains then you will love Arks of Octava. Let this fantasy world appease your inner child while the steamy scenes satisfy your adult fascinations!

Let me start by saying that fantasy is not where my heart lies when reading but I was so swept up in this book I could not put it down! I think every author has a signature and one of the many signatures of Lucian is his insane ability to write his characters. He makes those characters come to life and we all become endeared with each and every one of them.

Let me introduce you to some of the characters....

Jeremiah Poe is the main man Master Muse Rider from Octavia. He is a perfect mixture of serious, funny, protective and badass hot guy all rolled into one. His ramblings had me in a fit of laughter throughout quite a bit of the book.

Kane, cute amazing kid from the Horror province who Poe becomes very protective of. When Poe learns that Kane is experiencing real pain in Octava it leads them on a trip to Earth to find the other Arks and on the doorstep of one Scribbler who will steal both of their hearts.

Scribbler (Charlotte) is a funny character as well. Bane did an amazing job capturing the mind of a writer and bringing it to life with her. She and Poe have one thing in common..they both think romance and love are ridiculous but you have to see what happens when changes start to take place. Do they learn to love each other? Do they fight it tooth and nail and give you a very entertaining book?

So Arks of Octava has something for everyone. The kid in you wanting to take a journey to another world. The Adult who wants the steamy alpha male. The hopeless romantic wishing for the HEA for everyone. There is action, suspense, sex, drama and this story paints the most vivid of pictures in your mind while you read. I could see the beautiful music from the Sound Arks, the lights pouring from their instruments while Kane gave it his all as the conductor. I always say I know I have read a really good book when I can see the movie in my head..this was a fantastic movie!

My favorite quote from the book:

"Because of you, Contessant Juliet, I know life. I know pain. I know pleasure," he barely managed the hot rasp. "I know real." He lowered and kissed her trembling lips with a near hallowed reverence."I know the greatest love in all the realms. Because I know you."

5 out of 5 Stars!

While reading the blurbs and excerpts leading up to the book's release, I admit to being seriously confused as to what it was about. So, while I wasn't sure what to expect, 7% in, I know what I got - “Hill-Derky-Derk-Bomb-Pow”– pure genius!

The entire literary world is “brought to life” in “The Arks of Octava”. Genres, tropes, plots and characters each have been used to world-build the kingdom of Octava, and the results are phenomenal!

Octava is in the Scribbler's Realm, where characters written into existence by scribblers (writers/authors) exist while not being scribbled for. Jeremiah Poe, The Great Muse Rider, Changer of Lives and Definer of Destinies, and a Guardian, lives in Octava. He is also a Miskriat – a character who exists only in his Scribbler's mind, with no story written for him yet. So while he was a defined character, his story was not, and therein lies the source of his power (energy). Other story beings from various provinces (genres) approach him for help from time to time concerning matters outside of their storyline.

Befriending a small, scared boy, Kane, he found wandering in the Horror Province, is what leads Poe to actually step into a story to help the child. Kane and other children are being hurt INSIDE their stories – something that is expressly forbidden in Octava. Poe will have to travel outside the realm...to Earth...to retrieve the Seven Arks, to end the injustice. This also brings him face to face with his creator...his Scribbler, J.P. Howe – a woman - Charlotte!

There is quite a bit of fun...and confusion, as Poe and Charlotte figure out who they are, what is real, and how to go about finding the Seven Arks. Poe also has to deal with feelings, emotions and doubt for the first time in his short existence as coming to 'earth has made him human. Although she is human, Charlotte has to deal with the same issues, having blocked them out long ago and sequestered herself away in near isolation to write under a variety of pen names.

There is an evil chasing them as they begin their journey. An evil that does not want the revelations found out on Earth returned to Octava. Poe and Charlotte have to contend with the evil forces at hand, the lost memories of each found Ark...and each other.

The avid reader, and the passionate writer will devour this tale with glee! Lucian Bane has taken the strands of literature and woven them into a mesmerizing story that is “unputdownable!”

The story does not end in book 1, but there isn't really a cliffhanger – I hate those – but more a pause to regroup...and get ready.

“Arks of Octava: The Scribbler Guardian” is considered a Fantasy Romance, but I guarantee that no matter what your genres of preference are, you will enjoy this book!

Well, hot damn! That was a FRESH and wonderfully crafted tale. I had to spend a few days to formulate my thoughts on this one, figuring out how to put into words the FEELINGS marked into my heart and seeped into my soul after travelling in, and then out, of Poe’s universe. What sort of book was this? Well, it’s a mix of fantasy, adventure, and a sprinkling of romance thrown in as a wonderful and unexpected surprise.

If you love a good fantasy, and a story where the boundaries between worlds collide, then this is the take-me-away-adventure for you. The story reminds me of children walking through a wardrobe and stumbling into a magical world where a lion is king, of a precious girl sliding down a rabbit hole where nothing is what it seems, or a land where the magic of dreams comes alive for a boy and his flying ‘dog?’

In the Scribbler Guardian, heroes are front and center and the villains are very real, and oh so malevolent, yet understated in such a way as to make them even more vile. Such vibrant characters come to life in this book, it makes you stop … PAUSE … and go ‘Wait…could this in fact be real?’ Is it happening now?

That is the true wonder behind the gifted storytelling on these pages … The magic of this book is once you’re a few chapters in, you may just begin to believe the very premise behind the entire captivating world. As a novelist myself, I know I will never again write the same way again. The characters speaking to me to write their stories have always been alive to me, but now they have a home…PRECISELY BECAUSE OF THIS BOOK! They are ALIVE and BREATHE within the world of Octava.

So, let’s talk about some of those characters…

Let’s speak about Jeremiah Poe, the Master Muse Rider, a character who remains true to his Scribbler’s incarnation from beginning to end, and yet even then is capable of an intensity of growth and self-expression. He is a protector, a badass in his creation, he takes his role seriously, and endowed with the physical attributes of an alpha male, imbued with incredibly powerful sex appeal…an unexpected side effect of his Scribbler when he was created. Poe tackles his task with a single-minded determination, never once wavering from his goal, even when faced with an impossible and unwanted ending. He defends those weaker than himself, and it is in his interactions with the supporting characters where we see the soul created for Jeremiah Poe truly shine. His innocence is real. His sexiness is off the charts hot. The combination makes for some seriously explosive scenes.

Jeremiah Poe had me in awe from page one through the end, and yet there was another who stole the show from time to time. Kane, a cute and amazing genius-child from the Horror Province, brings all of Poe’s protective instincts to the forefront. When Poe learns Kane is experiencing true, horrific acts within his story, pain that shouldn’t be real in Octava, Poe is led on a journey to save this wonderful child.

The journey Poe and Kane take leads them directly to Poe’s creator, his Scribbler, a god-like figure who he reveres. Charlotte, a wonderfully funny and flawed woman, has one thing in common with the character she created but never finished writing. They both hate the messiness of romance… which sets up a wonderful romantic subplot to follow. For a woman who despises all things feminine and a man who believes love is a contagion, the path the two of them travel is wonderfully creative and magnificent, and steamy, dreamy, hot.

And, yet, there is a bad guy out there…and Poe is on a quest, one he cannot accomplish without the aid of Kane. Charlotte helps too; assisting Poe and Kane in their search, she navigates the foreign landscape of Earth. The three of them work together…most of the time.

The Scribbler Guardian has something for everyone … Fantasy … Adventure … Romance … Humor … a touch of Horror … and tons of fun. I felt transported into another realm. The kid in me enjoyed the fantasy. The hopeless romantic fawned over the steamy alpha male, and the action adventurer cheered the team closer to the final goal…

I would highly recommend this FIVE STAR book!
