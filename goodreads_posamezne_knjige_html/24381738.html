4 Broken and Battered Stars

“Abuse? Ah. Such problems, even with time, do not go away on their own. They must be addressed.”

My initial reaction to this read still stands....
My brain hurts. My emotions are all jumbled up.
Not at all what I expected and yet a very meaningful and heartfelt read.

Days later I find myself still analyzing my thoughts on this book. How the author was able to create such a gut wrenching story is beyond me. Abuse is moving on a deep emotional level. You must prepare yourself for the pain in order to appreciate the beauty in the healing. I feel it is critical to mention that this book includes many triggers. If you are a sensitive reader, you should be aware the content of this book includes .



"It seems to me that I deserve every single scar I have."

Grant is a physically and emotionally damaged man. He has plenty of money and a family and yet he is stuck in a deep state of depression. Fancy cars and plenty of money can't replace the heartbreak in his life. He seeks help by meeting with a therapist named Andre. Andre is able to tear down Grant's fortress of pain. When the secrets begin to unfold, they are heartbreaking.

"The outside of my body suits me. One-half looks fine; the other looks like Hell. Poetic justice, as I see it. Somehow, it seems like a direct reflection of what’s inside."

Andre is such an easy going man. He seems to be able to gauge when to push and when to pull. He offers a free flowing therapy and Grant begins to make progress towards healing.

“Take heart, my friend,” he says quietly, in his soothing velvet voice. “Do not despair. You simply move one step and then another. You keep going. Before you know it,” he snaps his fingers. “Voila. You have arrived. It took more than one day to arrive at this dark place, no? It will take more than one day to escape from here.”-Andre

One of the more striking realizations for me while reading this book was how abuse causes a ripple effect in the waters of a victim's life. The act might happen once and yet the after shock continues forever.

“Such a child must then live a lonely life of bitter uncertainty, constantly moving back and forth, between joy and despair.”-Andre



This book evokes so many questions. Why do people act the way that they do? Do we realize how deeply we effect others with our choices? Why do people abuse? What happens to the children and their children and so on and so on???

“All people, whatever they are doing, no matter how crazy or irrational it seems to you… it is how they need to act— from their perspective. I do not justify or rationalize an individual’s behavior— no. I simply tell you there is always a reason.”-Andre



“A child, without toxic interference, will naturally become the person they are meant to be.”-Andre

Andre has also offered healing to a young girl. Renata came to Andre after her family abandoned her. She had been living on the streets and was desperate to fill the void of her pain with sex. Andre offers her a home and a place to heal. Renata finds peace in working as a sexual therapist. I actually googled "sexual therapy" and found that most practices of that therapy is a "talk" type therapy and not a physical act done to help a person heal. However, Renata is employed by Andre to help people deal with their sexual hang ups. Of course, it would make sense for Andre to put Renata and Grant together.

“Listen to me very carefully. I have chosen to place two damaged people together in the hope they may heal each other.”-Andre

When Renata and Grant come together, it is painfully obvious there is going to be more between them then sex. For me, this is when the story really began to take off. Unfortunately, it was towards the end of the read. This only means I will have to continue to the next book if I want more of Grant and Renata.

Overall, Abuse was a very special read. I struggled through every single page because of the emotional pull it had my heart. The writing was fluid and yet the tempo was moderate to slow. I would have liked for there to have been more of Renata and Grant together and yet I suppose we needed every single word of this book in order to understand the true torment of these characters. I am sure that I will continue reading this series. I want more of Grant and Renata's story. There was a slight cliffy in this read and yet I didn't find it too bothersome. If you like an emotional and dark read then this is the book for you.



This was a Buddy Read with my girls from Turning Pages at Midnight. Thank you Stacie for picking out this read for our monthly TAG BR!

This is the first book in this trilogy, and it ends on a pretty big cliffhanger. The story is about Grant, a man who has lived with dark secrets and emotional pain since he can remember. Beyond the internal demons, he also has suffered gruesome burn marks that scare many people away from him on sight; as a result, he is convinced he is an unlovable, unworthy monster. He finds a therapist (Andre) who utilizes unusual, but effective, methods and feels working with him may be his only chance to live a somewhat normal life. As he opens up to Andre, his long held secrets begin to come to light, and Grant begins to believe that there may be a chance for him to move past these significant obstacles after all.

Andre has other clients, one of whom he has had in his care for many years: Renata. She came to him as a terrified, orphaned, unwanted 17 year old who did not speak at all and trusted no one. Over several years, Andre was able to get her to come more and more out of her shell to where she now serves as a sex surrogate for clients with specific hang-ups related to sex. Andre believes that Grant is one of these clients, so he connects the two and things progress from there.

What I liked:
--- Grant's story was interesting, and the way he revealed his secrets to Andre felt realistic for the type of person he seemed to be and the circumstances he was in
--- Andre is the most fascinating character in the book --- the author doesn't provide enough information about him to really understand what his background/motivations/training/etc. are yet; the mystery makes the reader want to continue on to explore this character more thoroughly

What I didn't like:
--- The way Renata's story was told felt choppy and repetitive; I didn't connect with her as much as I did with Grant
--- There were some things that just didn't make sense

This is a dark read with some heavy aspects and more than unsettling discussion/descriptions of the sexual, emotional, and physical abuse of underage children by trusted adults. If that offends you or might spark a trigger, please do not read.

Okay, this one started off really well. And for majority of the book actually. I loved how the facts, the clinical facts of Abuse in all forms, how it affects its victims is explained here. The emotional & just gut wrenching events were well written too.

What I have a problem with his how quickly, and I mean quickly the hero & heroine claim to love & be in love with one another after just one day.

Now I have no problems, usually, with insta love. It all depends on how it's delivered. But given the circumstances of both parties involved here, their own personal demons in the aftermath of the wrongs done to them, I find it EXTREMELY hard to believe they could recognize love in one another so quickly & with so little interaction before hand. I believe the intensity, the lust, all that to be there. But the love? NO. Not so soon.

And I also hate how this one was so rushed to come to a close. And it comes with a cliffy. But the way the story is written, how things unfold, you know it's coming long before it happens.
So I can't say that the "cliffy" was a cruel shock or unexpected, like some claim it to be.

But how you get from point A, B, and finally C, comes in just the last 2 chapters. So it makes it all feel choppy & rushed. Didn't like that. Almost like the author got tired of writing the story and just wanted to hurry up and finish it.

So this is why this read wound up with the 3.5 star rating and not the 5 star I saw it getting for majority of the story.

But there were many interesting elements here that I love. So I do plan to continue this series. I have to know how things turn out.

I would recommend this read. It's certainly not what you'd expect. And for the most part, that's a good thing.

I love, love, loved this! It's my second book in the year and it could do no wrong from start to finish.
"Abuse? Ah. Such problems, even with time, do not go away on their own. They must be addressed."

Abuse is my first book by Nikki Sex and it really blew me away! I will definitely be looking in to other books by the author because I just loved her writing style and story telling abilities. I was gripped right from the start, reading nearly half of it in one sitting.

So what's the gist of the story? Abuse is the story of two tortured souls, brought together by their friend/therapist, André Chevalier. Whilst the book isn't necessarily about André, I loved his character and would gladly read a book, or novella about him. Does one exist? :) His character was just so interesting and I really felt a connection to him whilst reading, regardless of the fact the book wasn't really 'his'. Onto the main characters..
"The actions and emotional responses of others are not your responsibility. You cannot save another from themselves. This is for them to do."

The book starts with us getting to know Grant Wilkinson. Like I said before, I was hooked from the start. Grant has lived through a terrible ordeal, on more than one occasion, and parts of his life are in tatters as a result. He seeks out a therapist, which is where he meets André and they form the most special of relationships. Not only are they therapist/patient, they start up a brilliant friendship. André helps Grant in so many ways. I loved their friendship, it was so interesting to read it go from strength to strength, thanks to the help André gave Grant. One way in which he helps, is to introduce him to Renata, a colleague of his, that has had her fair share of struggles in her life. Renata helps people, in a sexual way, and her and Grant are a match made in heaven. Which shocks them both. They don't 'meet' until further on in the book, but once they did I couldn't help but root for them.

Abuse is written in both Grant and Renata's POV, with a few chapters added in, in another POV, because of a side story going on. Which isn't complicated and very relevant. I admit I was a lot more interested in Grant's character, but that's purely because I found his story more fascinating than Renata's. "Facing one's past can be a perilous activity. For the client, joy must exceed misery. Personal successes must far outweigh losses. Pleasure must exceed pain. Always. Always. To do otherwise is a failure of the counselor."

There was a cliffhanger of sorts. Well, really, it was more that the side story is just left unfinished, so I'm interested to see if we are going to get a sequel. Hopefully so! All in all though, a really great book.
